My name is Shutian Liu (柳舒恬 in Chinese). I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics and Public Policy at [Imperial College Business School](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/business-school/), where I am fortunate to be advised by [Franco Sassi](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/people/f.sassi), [Verena Zuber](https://profiles.imperial.ac.uk/v.zuber), and [Pietro Biroli](https://sites.google.com/view/pietrobiroli/). I will be on the 2026&ndash;2027 academic job market. Before Imperial, I received my M.Sc. in Applied Economics and Management from Cornell University in 2020, and my B.Sc. in Management Science and B.Econ. in Economics from Renmin University of China in 2017. I am interested in health economics and genoeconomics, particularly how early-life environments shape the link between genetic predispositions and health, behaviour, and human capital outcomes.
